Getting There

The wedding ceremony and reception are at Point Reyes Seashore Lodge. You should fly into SFO or OAK and rent a car. For the wedding weekend, you'll want to stay in the Olema area; driving up from San Francisco takes about 2 hours.

There are two routes from SF to Olema:

  • Route 1 offers dramatic views of the Pacific as it winds along the coast, not unlike the famous Big Sur state park.
  • The faint of heart, heights, or time should take Sir Francis Drake Boulevard, which is also a lovely drive through golden hills and oak trees. If it looks familiar, it's because they film car ads around here!

Stuff to Do
Check the weather before you come, and bring warm clothes either way. June is a beautiful month here, but newbies are often surprised by how chilly it can be in northern California.

There's lots of cool stuff to do. Here are a few ideas. Locations and vendors are on the map below.

  • Drive out to Chimney Rock (the photo on our home page) and the Point Reyes Lighthouse.
  • Shuck and barbecue your own oysters at Drake's Bay Oyster Farm.
  • Sea kayak in Tomales Bay. There's some pretty sweet wildlife.
  • Take a stroll through Muir Woods, home of very large redwood trees.
  • Go hiking on Mount Tamalpais and take in 360-degree views of the entire San Francisco Bay and the Pacific Ocean.

Spending a few extra days in San Francisco? Here are some of our local-approved tourism tips:

  • Visit the California Academy of Sciences, a super cool aquarium and science museum.
  • Stroll through our neighborhood, the Mission District, and get some insane bread pudding at Tartine.
  • Eat at Shalimar, a hole-in-the-wall in a bad neighborhood with the best Indian food in SF.
  • Drive up Twin Peaks or Bernal Hill at night for a 360-degree view of the city.
  • Eat at Chez Panisse, mecca of California Cuisine.
  • Go watch the hang gliders at Fort Funston (highly weather dependant). You can even go on a tandem flight with our instructor!
